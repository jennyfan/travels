Montreal


A weekend of culinary indulgences in the beautiful Quebec summer

Breakfast poutine (sausage & eggs) at Patati Patata in Downtown Montreal
We ate three poutines within our first two hours in Montreal - this stringy, cheesy concoction was from Pitarifique
Dean got a tongue workout to make up for all of the cholesterol we were about to consume
A small refuge for ducks (as we learned, “le canard”) splits the clear surface of the lake in Parc du Mont-Royal
While not towering, the Mont du Royal offers a serene view of the city at the foot of its hills.

Four happy campers, one meh one

Montreal’s public pianos project was a charming way to integrate music into the landscape and invite musicians like Dean to participate
Dean ended up gaining a number of spectators beyond just Ten.
When the going gets tough, old man Ten likes to find a good chair to sit down on
We came for the poutine, but stayed for the bagels at St. Viateur (La Maison du Bagel), which were sweeter and smaller than its familiar New York cousin
Bagels, cream cheese, and lox were an extravagant yuppie mid-day snack, though the smaller sizes of the bagels saved us from an otherwise certain food coma
When you find a bro to sit with /thumbs up/
Beautiful mystery fruit unbeknownst to us before at the Marché Jean Talon — purple bell peppers and yellow cucumbers!

Produce in golden hour

It’s delicious!

Musical bikes in the Parc Casgrain & Shamrock — Montreal is covered with creative use of public spaces, like rocking-benches and musical installations for these stationary bikes
Trying to open a beer inventively with a key
The sparkling rosé wine is worth the effort
We cherry-picked a few interesting local beers to try while enjoying the rocking benches
Beer doesn’t make a picnic, so we ended the evening with a dinner of baguettes, ground cherries, duck rillettes and smoked duck, and clementines - yum!
The Festival du Mode (Fashion & Design Festival) near our Airbnb offered a “365 selfie” - in case you’re curious, the camera is spinning, not us!
These singing swings were part of the 21 Balançoise, a temporary musical swing set installation in the Quartiers des Spectacles of Montreal
These pink balls decked the entire street in Montreal’s Gay Village - yet another dynamic use of city space
Having gone to college in a city that boasts about its murals (Philadelphia), Montreal had no shortage of fun murals to beautify every block
Old Montreal was filled with the charm of European architecture and city streets, as well as talented balloon artists like this lady

Old Montreal - a lovely European experience on this side of the Atlantic

We came back to do this aerial ropes course later that night - how awesome does it look?
The Parc La Fontaine is a wonder in a summer evening — the serenity of being here instantly created this feeling of immense tranquility for all of its visitors

The perfect golden hour shot

A home-cooked meal of decadent foie gras poutine, duck-in-a-can from Au Pied de Cochon, and more fresh goods from the incredible grocers in Montreal!
